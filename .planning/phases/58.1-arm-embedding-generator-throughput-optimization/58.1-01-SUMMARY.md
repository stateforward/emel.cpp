# Phase 58.1 Summary

## Outcome

Phase 58.1 is complete. The maintained TE embedding generator keeps the already-landed ARM
throughput cleanup in place and now exposes steady-state `prepare`, `encode`, and `publish` timing
through the real embedding request surface used by the maintained benchmark runner.

## Delivered

- Added an observational benchmark timing payload to the public text, image, and audio embedding
  request events.
- Stamped the existing prepare, encode, and publish phase boundaries in
  `src/emel/embeddings/generator/actions.hpp` via timing helpers in `detail.hpp` without changing
  runtime routing or reintroducing helper-local control flow.
- Extended the dedicated embedding benchmark runner to report median
  `prepare_ns`, `encode_ns`, and `publish_ns` alongside total steady-state `ns_per_op`.
- Preserved the already-landed ARM hot-path work in the same maintained runtime:
  AArch64 kernel routing for steady-state f32/q8 matmuls, one-time fp16 expansion at bind time,
  and the image/audio buffer-swap flow that removes the worst full-buffer copy-backs.

## Measured Result

- Text steady-state: `5.709 ms/op` total, `~175.2 embeddings/s`
  with `prepare=0.0058 ms`, `encode=5.7033 ms`, `publish=0.00025 ms`.
- Audio steady-state: `55.571 ms/op` total, `~18.0 embeddings/s`
  with `prepare=29.974 ms`, `encode=25.521 ms`, `publish=0.00067 ms`.
- Image steady-state: `747.994 ms/op` total, `~1.34 embeddings/s`
  with `prepare=4.540 ms`, `encode=743.451 ms`, `publish=0.00079 ms`.

## Interpretation

- Text is overwhelmingly encode-bound and already close to a pure compute profile.
- Audio remains frontend-heavy; `prepare` is still a larger cost than `encode`.
- Image remains almost entirely encode-bound, confirming the remaining hot work is in the vision
  tower rather than preprocessing or publish.
